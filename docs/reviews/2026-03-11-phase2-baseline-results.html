3. Optimization Priority Ranking

Ordered by expected end-to-end impact, not by implementation convenience.

1. Reduce Hit-Test Cost First

Continuous interaction becomes the dominant cost from N=100 upward. At N=200, avg hit-test reaches 87.8970ms for 10% mutation and 106.1012ms for 50% mutation.

  • s100_m10_continuous: hit-test 34.6897ms, frame 76.1760ms
  • s200_m10_continuous: hit-test 87.8970ms, frame 184.2846ms
  • s200_m50_continuous: hit-test 106.1012ms, frame 224.9124ms

Recommended direction: Prioritize spatial index and broad-phase hit-test improvements before draw-path work. Continuous interaction cost is large enough to dominate total frame time.

2. Reduce Prepare/Rebuild Cost Next

Prepare cost scales steeply with scene size even when hit-testing is absent. None/occasional scenarios at N=100 and N=200 are already dominated by prepare time.

  • s100_m10_none: prepare 33.0710ms, draw 1.3986ms
  • s200_m10_none: prepare 81.5358ms, draw 1.9839ms
  • s200_m50_occasional: prepare 104.7756ms, draw 2.6080ms

Recommended direction: Prepared-scene caching, lower invalidation frequency, and cheaper engine.prepare() paths will move the needle much more than draw optimizations in baseline scenes.

3. Draw Cost Is Not The Primary Bottleneck

Across the whole matrix, draw stays relatively small: roughly 0.28ms to 2.61ms. It is consistently much lower than prepare and hit-test costs.

  • Highest avg draw row: s200_m50_occasional at 2.6080ms
  • Lowest avg draw row: s10_m50_continuous at 0.2758ms

Recommended direction: Path caching and native canvas can still help, but they are lower priority than prepare/hit-test work if the goal is largest end-to-end frame wins.

6. Practical Read

What the numbers mean in engineering terms.

Small scenes are cheap

N=10 stays close to vsync budget in every baseline scenario. Prepare stays around 1.48ms to 2.03ms, and continuous hit-test stays around 1.59ms to 1.63ms.

Hit-testing becomes dominant early

By N=50, continuous hit-testing already costs about 9.22ms to 10.42ms on average. By N=100 it reaches ~34ms, and by N=200 it reaches ~88ms to ~106ms.

Prepare dominates non-interactive baseline

With interaction removed, prepare still grows from ~7.75ms at N=50 to ~81.54ms to ~106.50ms at N=200. That makes prepare-path work the main baseline optimization lever.
